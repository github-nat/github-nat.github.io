EXPLORE THE IDENTITY SYSTEM

Our identity system is integral to our brand. It is responsible for creating a systematic visual language that compliments the brand mark. The system is comprised of many devices useful for creating flexibility, consistency and unity across applications.

TYPOGRAPHY

Typography plays a major role in shaping our brand. To create a consistent, engaging identity across all print applications, two standard families of typefaces were selected: Regular New and Din.

Personality typeface

Our personality typeface is Regular New. The two weights for use are Regular New Bold and Light. This typeface is primarily used for headings.

Primary typeface

Our primary typeface is Din. It can be used in four different weights: Light, Regular, Medium and Bold. This typeface is reserved for body copy.

Web typeface

Exo 2 is used for web communications.

System typeface

Arial is used in all MS Offices documents including email communication.

Signage typeface

Min Stencil is only used for internal building signage applications. It should only ever appear in uppercase.

COLOUR PALETTE

Colour plays an important role in the Transurban visual identity and accurate representation is essential. Each colour has a corresponding reference for print (Pantone and CMYK) and digital (RGB) applications.

Primary colour palette

The primary colour palette consists of TU green, TU black and TU grey.

Tonal palette

The tonal palette of TU green and TU grey is reserved for the graphic element.

Secondary colour palette

A secondary colour palette has been developed or use where a greater rangef of colours is required e.g. charts and diagrams.

GRAPHIC ELEMENT

Inspired by the curve of the road symbol in the brand mark, the core linear graphic element captures the idea of connection and continuity. It is available in a range of two colour and single colour versions.

Two-tone colour use

Use only on a duotone image (black and PMS417). This provides the best contrast and visibility of the graphic element.

Keyline white use

This version is applied to full colour imagery with an 80 per cent transparency.

Cropping and extending

Always ensure you capture both ends of the graphic element to fully communicate the idea of a journey and connection. It is also possible to extend or contract the width of the element while retaining the end curves.

Scale contrast

The full and cropped version of the graphic element can be combined as a flat graphic when photography is not required or relevant. This version uses scale contrast to achieve depth and visual interest.

Flipping

Flipping the graphic element horizontally can assist in creating an orientation to suit your layout. Rotating is not permitted.

PHOTOGRAPHY

Photography plays a vital role in conveying our brand personality to our customers and stakeholders, capturing the dynamic and changeable environment in which we operate. The collection is made up of three categories: hero (asset), dynamic movement and community.

Hero (asset)

Our hero (asset) photography features the scale and dramatic presence of road infrastructure assets. Reproduced in colour or duotones of TU black and TU grey the effect is bold, textural and refined.

Dynamic movement

This collection emphasises smooth movement and fast connections by adopting a slow-shutter speed effect.

Driver experience

The driver experience imagery has been created to capture the first person experience of being behind the wheel on the Transurban road network. The imagery captures the sense of a smooth and safe journey with its dynamic visual perspective.

Community

Transurban is passionate about positively impacting the communities in which we operate. This involvement is evidenced by an extensive collection of portraits and descriptive imagery representing our projects and community engagement. Reproduced in full colour or black and white overlaid with bold colours from the secondary colour palette the effect is engaging and dynamic.
